## Procedures

The placement process at NIT Calicut generally follows a structured procedure to ensure fairness and efficiency for both students and recruiting organizations. The overall process is coordinated by the Training & Placement Department.

```mermaid

    D --> E[Application & Shortlisting];

    subgraph Selection Process
```

**Explanation of the General Placement Procedure:**

1.  **T&P Registration:** Students interested in participating in campus placements must register with the Training & Placement Department, typically at the beginning of their final academic year. This involves providing academic details, resumes, and other required documentation.
2.  **Company Invitation & Job Announcement:** The T&P Dept invites companies to participate in the recruitment drive. Companies provide job descriptions, eligibility criteria, and salary packages, which are then communicated to registered students.
3.  **Pre-Placement Talk (PPT):** Companies often conduct PPTs on campus to introduce themselves, explain their work culture, job roles, and career growth opportunities to interested students. This session also allows students to clarify doubts.
4.  **Application & Shortlisting:** Students apply for roles they are interested in and meet the eligibility criteria. Companies then shortlist candidates based on academic performance, resumes, and sometimes preliminary assessments.
5.  **Selection Process:** Shortlisted candidates undergo a multi-stage selection process, which may include:
    *   **Written/Online Test:** Aptitude tests, technical tests, or coding challenges.
    *   **Group Discussion (GD):** To assess communication skills, teamwork, and problem-solving abilities.
    *   **Technical Interview:** To evaluate technical knowledge and problem-solving skills relevant to the job role.
    *   **HR Interview:** To assess personality, cultural fit, and general suitability for the company.
6.  **Offer Letter & Acceptance:** Successful candidates receive offer letters from the recruiting companies. Students are typically given a specific timeframe to accept or decline the offer.
7.  **Placement Concluded:** Upon acceptance, the placement process for that student with that company is concluded.

Specific rules regarding multiple offers, dream companies, and core placements are typically outlined in the official placement policy document released by the T&P Department each year.
