# Graduation at NIT Calicut

## Overview

Graduation at the National Institute of Technology Calicut (NIT Calicut) marks the formal conferral of academic degrees to students who have successfully completed the requirements of their respective programs. This significant academic milestone is typically celebrated through an annual Convocation Ceremony, where degrees are officially awarded by the institute. The ceremony serves as a formal recognition of the graduates' academic achievements and their transition from students to alumni.

## Details

### Degrees Awarded

NIT Calicut awards various undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral degrees upon successful completion of the prescribed academic curricula. These include:
*   Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech)
*   Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch)
*   Master of Technology (M.Tech)
*   Master of Planning (M.Plan)
*   Master of Computer Applications (MCA)
*   Master of Business Administration (MBA)
*   Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.)

### Eligibility

Eligibility for graduation and participation in the Convocation Ceremony is generally extended to students who have:
*   Successfully completed all academic requirements for their respective degree programs.
*   Cleared all examinations and submitted all necessary academic work (e.g., theses, project reports).
*   Settled all financial dues and cleared any outstanding liabilities with the institute.
*   Met any specific deadlines or criteria set by the institute for a particular academic year's convocation.

### Frequency and Timing

The Convocation Ceremony at NIT Calicut is typically held annually. The exact date usually falls in the latter part of the calendar year (e.g., November or December) or early the following year, depending on the academic calendar and administrative scheduling. Specific dates are announced well in advance by the institute.

## History

Detailed historical records regarding the exact date of the first convocation ceremony or significant historical changes to the graduation process at NIT Calicut are not readily available in public sources. However, as an institute of national importance established in 1961 (initially as Calicut Regional Engineering College), NIT Calicut has a long-standing tradition of formally graduating its students annually since its inception.

## Facilities

The Convocation Ceremony at NIT Calicut is typically held at the institute's main auditorium or a designated large venue within the campus, capable of accommodating graduates, faculty, dignitaries, and guests. The specific venue may vary based on the scale of the event and logistical considerations for a given year.

## Procedures

The general procedure for students participating in the Convocation Ceremony at NIT Calicut typically involves several steps, communicated by the institute to eligible graduates. While specific details may vary slightly from year to year, the core process generally follows this sequence:

```mermaid
graph TD
    A[Completion of Degree Requirements] --> B{Eligibility Check by Institute};
    B -- Eligible --> C[Official Convocation Announcement];
    C --> D[Online Registration for Convocation];
    D --> E[Payment of Convocation Fee];
    E --> F[Collection of Academic Robes/Gowns];
    F --> G[Attendance at Convocation Rehearsal (If Scheduled)];
    G --> H[Attendance at Convocation Ceremony];
    H --> I[Degree Conferral];
    I --> J[Collection of Degree Certificate];
```

**Explanation of Steps:**
1.  **Completion of Degree Requirements:** Students must successfully complete all academic and administrative requirements for their respective degree programs.
2.  **Eligibility Check by Institute:** The academic section of NIT Calicut verifies the eligibility of students based on their academic records and clearance status.
3.  **Official Convocation Announcement:** The institute formally announces the date, time, and other details of the upcoming Convocation Ceremony.
4.  **Online Registration for Convocation:** Eligible students are required to register online through a dedicated portal to confirm their participation in the ceremony.
5.  **Payment of Convocation Fee:** A prescribed fee must be paid by participating students as part of the registration process.
6.  **Collection of Academic Robes/Gowns:** Graduates are typically required to collect academic robes or gowns for the ceremony, often from a designated counter on campus.
7.  **Attendance at Convocation Rehearsal:** A rehearsal may be conducted prior to the main ceremony to familiarize graduates with the proceedings. Attendance might be optional or mandatory depending on institute directives.
8.  **Attendance at Convocation Ceremony:** Graduates attend the formal ceremony, which includes addresses by dignitaries, the presentation of degrees, and the oath-taking.
9.  **Degree Conferral:** Degrees are formally conferred upon the graduates by the Chairman of the Board of Governors or the Director of the institute.
10. **Collection of Degree Certificate:** Degree certificates are typically distributed either during the ceremony itself or made available for collection at a later date from the academic section.
