## History

NIT Calicut, formerly known as Calicut Regional Engineering College (CREC), was established in 1961. Its transformation into an Institute of National Importance in 2002 under the NIT Act significantly bolstered its research mandate and capabilities. This upgrade led to increased funding, autonomy, and an emphasis on postgraduate studies and research. Over the years, the institute has progressively strengthened its research infrastructure, faculty expertise, and collaborations, evolving into a recognized center for technical education and research in India. The establishment of dedicated research centers and the growth of its Ph.D. programs mark key milestones in its research journey.

## Procedures

The research activities at NIT Calicut are generally managed and facilitated by the Dean (Research & Consultancy) office and the Research & Development (R&D) Cell. The procedures for initiating, funding, and managing research projects typically follow a structured process.

A general representation of the lifecycle for sponsored research projects at NIT Calicut is as follows:

```mermaid
graph TD
    A[Faculty/Researcher identifies Research Opportunity] --> B{Proposal Preparation};
    B --> C[Departmental Scrutiny Committee/Head of Department];
    C -- Approval/Recommendations --> D[Dean (Research & Consultancy) Office];
    D -- Endorsement/Review --> E[Submission to External Funding Agency/Industry];
    E -- Grant Awarded --> F[Project Implementation & Management (with R&D Cell Support)];
    F --> G[Research Output (Publications, Patents, Reports, Prototypes)];
    G --> H[Dissemination & Impact Assessment];
    C -- Revisions Needed --> B;
    D -- Revisions Needed --> B;
    E -- Not Awarded --> B;
```

**Explanation of the Process:**

1.  **Identification of Opportunity:** Faculty members identify research problems or funding opportunities from various national/international agencies or industries.
2.  **Proposal Preparation:** A detailed research proposal is prepared outlining objectives, methodology, budget, and expected outcomes.
3.  **Departmental Scrutiny:** The proposal undergoes review by a Departmental Scrutiny Committee or the Head of the Department to ensure academic rigor and alignment with departmental goals.
4.  **Dean (Research & Consultancy) Office Review:** The Dean (R&C) office reviews the proposal for institutional compliance, resource availability, and overall feasibility before providing institutional endorsement.
5.  **Submission to Funding Agency:** The endorsed proposal is submitted to the relevant external funding agency or industry partner.
6.  **Project Implementation & Management:** Upon successful grant award, the project is initiated. The R&D Cell typically assists with administrative, financial, and logistical aspects of project management.
7.  **Research Output:** The project generates various outputs, including scientific publications, patents, technical reports, and potentially prototypes or software.
8.  **Dissemination & Impact:** Research findings are disseminated through academic channels, and efforts are made to assess and maximize the societal or industrial impact of the research.

Similar structured procedures are in place for consultancy projects and intellectual property filing, involving relevant institutional bodies to ensure compliance and support.
