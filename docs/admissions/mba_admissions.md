# MBA Admissions at NIT Calicut

## Overview

The Master of Business Administration (MBA) program at the National Institute of Technology Calicut (NIT Calicut) is a two-year, full-time postgraduate degree administered by the School of Management Studies (SOMS). The program is designed to equip students with management knowledge and skills relevant to various industries. Admission to the MBA program is competitive and is based on a combination of national-level management aptitude test scores, academic performance, and performance in subsequent selection rounds.

## Details

### Program Structure
The MBA program at NIT Calicut is a two-year, full-time residential program. It typically follows a semester system, with a curriculum that includes core management subjects and elective courses.

### Eligibility Criteria
Candidates seeking admission to the MBA program must generally meet the following criteria:
*   **Educational Qualification:** A Bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognized university or institution.
*   **Minimum Marks:** A minimum aggregate percentage or CGPA in the qualifying Bachelor's degree. This typically ranges from 60% or 6.5 CGPA (on a 10-point scale) for General/EWS/OBC candidates, and 55% or 6.0 CGPA for SC/ST/PwD candidates. Specific percentages may vary by admission cycle.
*   **Entrance Examination:** A valid score in one of the accepted national-level management aptitude tests.

### Accepted Entrance Examinations
NIT Calicut generally accepts scores from the following management entrance examinations:
*   Common Admission Test (CAT)
*   Common Management Admission Test (CMAT)
*   Xavier Aptitude Test (XAT)
*   Management Aptitude Test (MAT)
*   Graduate Management Admission Test (GMAT)

### Intake Capacity
The sanctioned intake capacity for the MBA program at NIT Calicut is typically 60 students per academic year. This number is subject to change based on regulatory approvals and institutional policies.

### Selection Process
The selection process for the MBA program at NIT Calicut involves multiple stages:
1.  **Shortlisting:** Candidates are shortlisted based on their valid scores in the accepted management entrance examinations.
2.  **Group Discussion (GD) and Personal Interview (PI):** Shortlisted candidates are invited to participate in a Group Discussion and Personal Interview round.
3.  **Final Merit List:** A final merit list is prepared based on a comprehensive evaluation, which typically includes:
    *   Score in the management entrance examination.
    *   Performance in Group Discussion.
    *   Performance in Personal Interview.
    *   Academic record (10th, 12th, and Bachelor's degree).
    *   Work experience (if any).
    *   Other relevant parameters as decided by the institute for the specific admission cycle.

The exact weightage assigned to each component may vary from year to year and is published in the official admission notification for that particular cycle.

## History

The School of Management Studies (SOMS) at NIT Calicut was established in 2008. Since its inception, SOMS has been offering the Master of Business Administration (MBA) program, contributing to management education and research within the NIT system.

## Facilities

The School of Management Studies (SOMS) utilizes the general infrastructure and facilities available at NIT Calicut. Specific facilities dedicated to the MBA program and SOMS typically include:
*   **Classrooms:** Dedicated classrooms equipped with audio-visual aids.
*   **Computer Lab:** A computer laboratory with internet access and relevant software.
*   **Departmental Library:** A collection of management-related books, journals, and other resources, supplementing the central library.
*   **Central Library:** Access to the extensive collection of books, e-journals, databases, and other learning resources available at the NIT Calicut Central Library.
*   **Hostel Accommodation:** Residential facilities are available for MBA students within the NIT Calicut campus.

Specific details regarding exclusive facilities for SOMS beyond these general provisions are not consistently published in public domain.
