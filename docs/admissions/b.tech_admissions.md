# B.Tech Admissions at NIT Calicut

## Overview
B.Tech admissions at the National Institute of Technology Calicut (NITC) are primarily conducted through a centralized counseling process based on national-level entrance examinations. The institute admits students to its undergraduate Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) programs across various engineering disciplines. Admissions are governed by policies set by the Ministry of Education, Government of India, and are managed by designated national agencies.

## Details

### Eligibility Criteria (Indian Nationals)
Admission for Indian nationals to B.Tech programs at NIT Calicut is primarily based on the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main. The general eligibility criteria include:
*   **JEE (Main) Qualification:** Candidates must have appeared for JEE (Main) and secured a valid All India Rank (AIR).
*   **Academic Qualification:** Candidates must have passed the 10+2 (or equivalent) examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as compulsory subjects.
*   **Minimum Marks:** Candidates must have secured at least 75% aggregate marks in the 10+2 examination, or be in the top 20 percentile of their respective qualifying examination board. For Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), and Persons with Disability (PwD) candidates, the minimum aggregate marks requirement is 65%.

### Eligibility Criteria (Foreign Nationals/NRIs/PIOs/OCIs - DASA/CIWG Scheme)
NIT Calicut also admits foreign nationals, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), Persons of Indian Origin (PIOs), and Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs) under the Direct Admission of Students Abroad (DASA) scheme, which includes a sub-scheme for Children of Indian Workers in Gulf Countries (CIWG). The eligibility criteria typically include:
*   **Academic Qualification:** Candidates must have passed the 10+2 (or equivalent) examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
*   **Standardized Test:** Candidates are generally required to have a valid score in the SAT Subject Tests (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics Level 2) or, as per recent changes, the SAT Reasoning Test. Specific score requirements are announced annually by the DASA coordinating NIT.
*   **Citizenship/Residency:** Specific criteria regarding nationality and residency status apply as defined by the DASA scheme.

### Reservation Policy
NIT Calicut adheres to the reservation policies of the Government of India. Seats are reserved for various categories, including:
*   Scheduled Castes (SC)
*   Scheduled Tribes (ST)
*   Other Backward Classes – Non-Creamy Layer (OBC-NCL)
*   Economically Weaker Sections (EWS)
*   Persons with Disability (PwD)
*   Female candidates (Supernumerary seats as per JoSAA/CSAB guidelines)

### Available Programs
NIT Calicut offers B.Tech degrees in various disciplines. The specific programs offered may vary slightly from year to year but generally include:
*   Biotechnology
*   Chemical Engineering
*   Civil Engineering
*   Computer Science and Engineering
*   Electrical and Electronics Engineering
*   Electronics and Communication Engineering
*   Engineering Physics
*   Materials Science and Engineering
*   Mechanical Engineering
*   Production Engineering

## Procedures

### Admission Procedure for Indian Nationals (JEE Main & JoSAA/CSAB)
The admission process for Indian nationals is centralized and managed by the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) and subsequently by the Central Seat Allocation Board (CSAB).

```mermaid
graph TD
    A[Appear for JEE (Main) Exam] --> B{Qualify JEE (Main)?};
    B -- Yes --> C[Register for JoSAA Counseling];
    C --> D[Fill and Lock Choices (NIT Calicut Programs)];
    D --> E[JoSAA Seat Allotment Rounds];
    E -- Allotted Seat --> F[Accept Seat & Pay Fee];
    F --> G[Document Verification at Reporting Centre];
    G --> H{Seat Confirmed?};
    H -- Yes --> I[Physical Reporting at NIT Calicut];
    H -- No (Withdraw/Float) --> E;
    E -- No Allotment after JoSAA --> J{Vacant Seats Remaining?};
    J -- Yes --> K[Register for CSAB Special Rounds];
    K --> L[CSAB Seat Allotment];
    L -- Allotted Seat --> M[Accept Seat & Pay Fee (CSAB)];
    M --> N[Document Verification (CSAB)];
    N --> O[Physical Reporting at NIT Calicut];
    J -- No --> P[Admission Cycle Concluded];
```

### Admission Procedure for Foreign Nationals/NRIs/PIOs/OCIs (DASA/CIWG Scheme)
The admission process for candidates under the DASA/CIWG scheme is managed by a coordinating NIT designated by the Ministry of Education.

```mermaid
graph TD
    A[Appear for SAT (or equivalent) Exam] --> B[Check DASA/CIWG Eligibility Criteria];
    B --> C[Apply Online through DASA Portal];
    C --> D[Upload Required Documents & Pay Application Fee];
    D --> E[DASA Merit List & Seat Allotment Rounds];
    E -- Allotted Seat --> F[Accept Seat & Pay Fee (DASA)];
    F --> G[Document Verification (Online/Physical as per DASA)];
    G --> H[Physical Reporting at NIT Calicut];
    E -- No Allotment --> I[Admission Cycle Concluded];
```

## History
NIT Calicut was established in 1961 as Calicut Regional Engineering College (CREC), one of the first Regional Engineering Colleges (RECs) in India. Initially, admissions were primarily based on state-level engineering entrance examinations and later through a national-level examination for RECs. In 2002, CREC was upgraded to a National Institute of Technology (NIT) and granted deemed university status, becoming NIT Calicut. With this transition, the admission process shifted to the All India Engineering Entrance Examination (AIEEE). Subsequently, AIEEE was replaced by the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main in 2013. The centralized counseling process through JoSAA and CSAB was introduced to streamline admissions to NITs, IITs, IIITs, and GFTIs, ensuring a unified and transparent system.

## Facilities
Information regarding dedicated physical facilities solely for the admission process, separate from the general administrative and academic infrastructure of NIT Calicut, is not publicly detailed. During the physical reporting and admission formalities, candidates utilize the institute's existing administrative blocks, auditoriums, and computer labs as designated by the institute for the purpose. Online admission processes are facilitated through national portals (JoSAA, CSAB, DASA) and the institute's official website.
