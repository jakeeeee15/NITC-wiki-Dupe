# Scholarships at NIT Calicut

## Overview

National Institute of Technology Calicut (NIT Calicut) students are eligible for various scholarships and financial assistance schemes. These opportunities primarily aim to support students from economically weaker sections, specific social categories, or those demonstrating academic merit. The majority of scholarships available to students at NIT Calicut are administered by central and state governments, with some additional support potentially provided through institute initiatives or alumni contributions.

## Details

Scholarships at NIT Calicut can broadly be categorized based on their funding source and eligibility criteria:

### 1. Government Scholarships

Students enrolled at NIT Calicut are eligible for a wide array of scholarships offered by various ministries of the Government of India and respective state governments. These are typically processed through the National Scholarship Portal (NSP) or state-specific portals.

*   **Central Sector Schemes:**
    *   **Post Matric Scholarships:** For SC, ST, OBC, and Minority students.
    *   **Top Class Education Scheme for SC Students:** Administered by the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment.
    *   **Top Class Education Scheme for ST Students:** Administered by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs.
    *   **Prime Minister's Scholarship Scheme (PMSS):** For wards of Ex-servicemen.
    *   **Scholarships for Students with Disabilities (Divyangjan):** Administered by the Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities.
    *   **AICTE Pragati Scholarship Scheme:** For girl students pursuing technical education.
    *   **AICTE Saksham Scholarship Scheme:** For specially-abled students pursuing technical education.
    *   **MHRD Post Graduate Scholarship for GATE/GPAT Qualified Students:** For M.Tech/M.Plan/M.Arch students admitted through GATE/GPAT.
*   **State Government Scholarships:** Students domiciled in various states may be eligible for scholarships offered by their respective state governments, provided they meet the specific eligibility criteria set by those states. Examples include scholarships from the Government of Kerala for its residents.

Eligibility criteria, application timelines, and scholarship amounts for government schemes are determined by the respective funding bodies and are subject to change.

### 2. Institute-Specific Scholarships

Information regarding specific institute-funded scholarships directly administered by NIT Calicut, beyond the general fee waivers or teaching assistantships for M.Tech/Ph.D. students, is not extensively detailed in publicly available sources. However, NITs, as centrally funded technical institutions, often provide:

*   **Teaching Assistantships (TA):** For M.Tech and Ph.D. students, often linked to their admission through GATE/NET, which includes a monthly stipend and tuition fee waiver.
*   **Merit-cum-Means Scholarships:** While not always explicitly named as "scholarships," NITs may offer fee concessions or waivers based on parental income and academic performance, as per Ministry of Education guidelines.

### 3. Alumni and Private Scholarships

The NIT Calicut Alumni Association (NITCAA) and other private organizations or trusts may occasionally offer scholarships to deserving students. Details of such scholarships, including eligibility and application procedures, are typically announced by the institute or the respective organizations when available. Specific, regularly recurring alumni-funded scholarship programs are not consistently detailed in public domain information.

## History

Specific historical details regarding the establishment and evolution of scholarship programs exclusively at NIT Calicut are not extensively documented in publicly available sources. The availability of scholarships at NIT Calicut has largely mirrored the introduction and expansion of national and state-level scholarship schemes for higher education in India, particularly for students in Centrally Funded Technical Institutions (CFTIs).

## Facilities

NIT Calicut typically designates a section within its Student Affairs or Academic office to assist students with scholarship-related queries, document verification, and application processes. This office acts as a liaison between students and the various scholarship-providing bodies (e.g., National Scholarship Portal, state departments). A dedicated, distinct "Scholarship Facility" building or department is not explicitly detailed in public information.

## Procedures

The procedures for applying for scholarships vary depending on the type of scholarship.

### 1. Government Scholarships (via National Scholarship Portal - NSP)

Most central government scholarships are applied for through the National Scholarship Portal (NSP). The general procedure is as follows:

```mermaid
graph TD
    A[Scholarship Announcement/Portal Open] --> B{Student identifies eligible scheme on NSP};
    B -- Yes --> C[Registers/Logs in on NSP];
    C --> D[Fills application form online];
    D --> E[Uploads required documents (e.g., income certificate, caste certificate, academic records)];
    E --> F[Submits application online];
    F --> G[Application forwarded to NIT Calicut for verification];
    G --> H[NIT Calicut verifies student details & documents];
    H -- Verified --> I[Application forwarded to State/Central Nodal Officer];
    I --> J[Scholarship sanctioned & disbursed directly to student's bank account];
    B -- No --> K[Student explores other scholarship options];
```

**Key Steps:**
*   **Registration:** New users register on the NSP portal.
*   **Application Filling:** Complete the online application form with personal, academic, and bank details.
*   **Document Upload:** Upload scanned copies of necessary documents as specified by the scholarship scheme.
*   **Institute Verification:** NIT Calicut's designated office verifies the student's enrollment, academic performance, and submitted documents.
*   **Disbursement:** Once approved by the respective ministry/department, the scholarship amount is typically disbursed directly to the student's Aadhaar-linked bank account.

### 2. Institute/Alumni/Private Scholarships

Procedures for institute-specific, alumni-funded, or private scholarships, if available, are announced by NIT Calicut or the respective funding body. These typically involve:

*   **Announcement:** Notification through the institute's website, notice boards, or student email.
*   **Application:** Submission of a physical or online application form to a designated office (e.g., Student Affairs, Dean Academics office).
*   **Document Submission:** Providing required documents such as academic transcripts, income proofs, and letters of recommendation.
*   **Selection:** Evaluation by a scholarship committee based on merit, need, or specific criteria.
*   **Disbursement:** Direct transfer to the student's account or adjustment against fees.

Specific details for these types of scholarships are not consistently available in public domain and may vary each academic year. Students are advised to regularly check the official NIT Calicut website and notice boards for updates.
