# Fee Structure of NIT Calicut

## Overview

The fee structure at the National Institute of Technology Calicut (NIT Calicut) is determined by the Ministry of Education (MoE), Government of India, and is subject to periodic revisions. It comprises various components, including academic fees, hostel fees, mess charges, and other institutional charges. The structure varies based on the program of study (e.g., B.Tech, M.Tech, PhD), the student's category (e.g., General, OBC, SC, ST, PwD, EWS), and family income.

NIT Calicut adheres to the fee waiver policies mandated by the Government of India for students from specific socio-economic backgrounds. All fees are typically payable per semester, with a refundable caution deposit collected at the time of admission.

## Details

The fee structure is broadly categorized into academic fees, hostel fees, and other charges. Specific amounts are subject to change based on official notifications from the Institute or the Ministry of Education. Students are advised to refer to the latest admission brochures and official fee notices published on the NIT Calicut website for the most current and accurate figures.

### Components of Fees

The typical components of the fee structure include:

1.  **Tuition Fee:** The primary academic charge.
2.  **Admission Fee:** A one-time fee paid at the time of admission.
3.  **Institute Development Fee:** Contributes to the development and maintenance of institutional infrastructure.
4.  **Library Fee:** Covers access to library resources and services.
5.  **Computer/Internet Fee:** For access to computing facilities and internet services.
6.  **Examination Fee:** Charged for conducting examinations.
7.  **Student Activity Fee:** Supports various student welfare and extracurricular activities.
8.  **Medical Fee:** For basic medical services available on campus.
9.  **Hostel Rent:** Charges for accommodation in institute hostels.
10. **Mess Charges:** Covers food services provided by the hostel mess. These are often managed by student committees and are subject to variation based on actual consumption and operational costs.
11. **Caution Deposit:** A refundable deposit collected at the time of admission, returned upon completion of the program after deductions for any damages or outstanding dues.

### Program-wise Variation

The fee structure varies significantly across different programs:

*   **Undergraduate Programs (B.Tech):** Generally have a standardized tuition fee, subject to waivers.
*   **Postgraduate Programs (M.Tech, MCA, MBA):** Tuition fees for these programs may differ from undergraduate programs. M.Tech students admitted through CCMT with valid GATE scores may be eligible for scholarships/stipends from the MoE.
*   **Doctoral Programs (PhD):** Tuition fees for PhD programs may also vary. Research scholars may be eligible for fellowships or assistantships.

### Category-wise Variation and Fee Waivers

NIT Calicut implements the following fee waiver policies as per Government of India directives:

*   **SC/ST/PwD Students:** Full waiver of the Tuition Fee.
*   **General/OBC/EWS Students with Family Income < ₹1 Lakh per Annum:** Full waiver of the Tuition Fee.
*   **General/OBC/EWS Students with Family Income between ₹1 Lakh and ₹5 Lakhs per Annum:** Two-thirds (2/3rd) waiver of the Tuition Fee.
*   **General/OBC/EWS Students with Family Income > ₹5 Lakhs per Annum:** Full Tuition Fee is applicable.

Other fees (e.g., Admission Fee, Institute Development Fee, Library Fee, Computer/Internet Fee, Examination Fee, Student Activity Fee, Medical Fee, Hostel Rent, Mess Charges, Caution Deposit) are generally applicable to all students, irrespective of category or income, unless specifically exempted by an official notification.

## Facilities

The fee structure primarily covers the costs associated with academic instruction, residential accommodation, and general institutional services. Specific fees like the Library Fee, Computer/Internet Fee, and Medical Fee directly contribute to the maintenance and provision of these respective facilities. Other facilities, such as sports complexes, laboratories, and workshops, are supported through broader institutional development and maintenance funds, which are partially covered by the Institute Development Fee. There are no separate, itemized fees for individual facilities beyond these general categories.
