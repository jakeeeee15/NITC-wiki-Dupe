# Examinations at NIT Calicut

## Overview

Examinations at the National Institute of Technology Calicut (NIT Calicut) form an integral part of the academic evaluation system for all undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs. The primary objective of these examinations is to assess students' understanding of course material, their analytical abilities, and their capacity to apply learned concepts. The evaluation system typically comprises a combination of continuous internal evaluation (CIE) and end-semester examinations (ESE).

## Details

The academic performance of students in each course is generally evaluated through a two-pronged approach:

*   **Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE):** This component assesses student learning throughout the semester. It typically includes various elements such as mid-semester examinations, quizzes, assignments, laboratory work, projects, presentations, and seminars. The specific components and their weightage are determined by the respective course faculty and department, in accordance with the institute's academic regulations.
*   **End Semester Examination (ESE):** This is a comprehensive examination conducted at the end of each academic semester. It is typically a written examination designed to evaluate the student's overall grasp of the course content. The duration of the ESE is usually three hours.

**Weightage:**
While specific weightages can vary slightly between programs or courses, a common distribution observed in the institute's academic regulations is approximately 40% for CIE and 60% for ESE. The exact weightage for each component is specified in the academic regulations for the respective program.

**Grading System:**
NIT Calicut employs a letter grading system. Each letter grade corresponds to a specific range of marks and carries a certain number of grade points. The common letter grades include:
*   A+ (Outstanding)
*   A (Excellent)
*   B+ (Very Good)
*   B (Good)
*   C+ (Above Average)
*   C (Average)
*   D (Pass)
*   F (Fail)
*   W (Withdrawal)
*   I (Incomplete)

The academic performance is quantified using the Semester Grade Point Average (SGPA) for each semester and the Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) for the entire program. These are calculated based on the grade points earned in all courses.

**Passing Criteria:**
To pass a course, students are generally required to secure a minimum percentage of marks in the End Semester Examination (ESE) and a minimum overall percentage of marks (CIE + ESE combined). The specific minimum percentages are defined in the institute's academic regulations.

### Examination Registration

Students are required to register for their End Semester Examinations within a stipulated timeframe announced by the Controller of Examinations. This typically involves verifying their course registrations and clearing any outstanding dues.

### Conduct of Examinations

*   **Admit Cards:** Students must possess a valid admit card to appear for the ESE.
*   **Invigilation:** Examinations are conducted under strict invigilation to ensure fairness and prevent malpractice.
*   **Malpractice:** Any form of academic malpractice during examinations is dealt with severely as per the institute's anti-malpractice rules, which may include debarment from examinations or expulsion.

### Result Publication

Results for End Semester Examinations are typically published on the institute's official website and/or student portals within a few weeks after the examinations conclude.

### Re-evaluation and Re-totaling

Students who are dissatisfied with their ESE results may apply for re-evaluation or re-totaling of their answer scripts. This process is subject to specific deadlines and payment of a prescribed fee.
