# Course Registration at NIT Calicut

## Overview

Course registration at the National Institute of Technology Calicut (NIT Calicut) is a mandatory academic process through which students formally enroll in courses for an upcoming academic semester. This process ensures that students are registered for the required number of credits, adhere to program-specific curricula, and fulfill prerequisites for their chosen courses. The specific dates and deadlines for course registration, including pre-registration, main registration, and add/drop periods, are published in the official Academic Calendar for each academic year and program. The process is primarily conducted through an online academic management system.

## Details

The course registration process at NIT Calicut is governed by the Institute's academic regulations, which outline the policies and procedures for various aspects of student enrollment. While specific regulations may vary slightly across different programs (e.g., B.Tech, M.Tech, MCA, MBA, PhD), the fundamental principles remain consistent. The following details are primarily based on the "Regulations for B.Tech. Degree Programme (Effective from 2019 Admissions)":

*   **Types of Registration:**
    *   **Pre-registration:** Students are typically required to pre-register for courses of the next semester during the current semester. This helps in planning and resource allocation.
    *   **Main Registration:** Formal registration for courses occurs at the beginning of each semester.
    *   **Late Registration:** Students who miss the specified registration dates may be permitted to register during a late registration period, usually upon payment of a late registration fee.
*   **Course Load:** Students are generally required to register for a minimum and maximum number of credits per semester. For B.Tech students, this is typically a maximum of 28 credits and a minimum of 16 credits in a regular semester. In the final semester, a minimum of 12 credits may be permitted.
*   **Prerequisites:** Registration for a course is contingent upon the successful completion of its specified prerequisite courses as outlined in the curriculum. The online system typically enforces these prerequisites.
*   **Add/Drop of Courses:** Students are allowed to add or drop courses within a specified period at the beginning of the semester, as indicated in the Academic Calendar.
*   **Withdrawal from a Course:** A student may withdraw from a registered course within a designated period. Withdrawal from a course is generally permitted only once per course.
*   **Registration for Specific Course Types:** The regulations also cover registration for various types of courses, including:
    *   Backlog Courses
    *   Project/Thesis
    *   Internship
    *   Audit Courses
    *   Elective Courses (Departmental Electives, Open Electives)
    *   MOOC (Massive Open Online Courses) Courses
    *   Fast Track Courses
    *   Summer Term Courses

## Facilities

The primary facility for course registration at NIT Calicut is the Institute's online academic management system or student portal. This web-based platform allows students to select courses, view their academic records, and manage their registration status.

For queries, approvals, or specific issues that cannot be resolved online, students may interact with:
*   Their respective Faculty Advisors.
*   The Head of their academic Department.
*   The Academic Section of the Institute.

## Procedures

The general procedure for course registration at NIT Calicut involves several stages, typically initiated by the publication of the Academic Calendar. The process is largely online, with specific deadlines for each stage.

```mermaid
graph TD
    A[Academic Calendar Published] --> B{Student Reviews Course Offerings & Prerequisites};
    B --> C[Pre-registration Period Begins];
    C --> D[Student Selects Tentative Courses Online];
    D --> E[Main Registration Period Begins];
    E --> F{Student Confirms Course Selection & Pays Fees};
    F --> G{Course Registration Complete};
    G --> H[Add/Drop Period];
    H --> I{Student Modifies Course Selection (if needed)};
    I --> J[Withdrawal Period];
    J --> K{Student Withdraws from Course (if needed)};
    E -- Late Registration Fee --> L[Late Registration Period];
    L --> F;
```

**Explanation of Stages:**

1.  **Academic Calendar Publication:** The Institute publishes the Academic Calendar, which specifies the dates for pre-registration, main registration, add/drop, and withdrawal periods for the upcoming semester.
2.  **Course Review:** Students are expected to review the courses offered for the semester, understand their curriculum requirements, and check prerequisites for desired courses.
3.  **Pre-registration:** During this period, students log into the online portal to select the courses they intend to take in the upcoming semester. This helps departments gauge demand.
4.  **Main Registration:** At the beginning of the semester, students finalize their course selections on the online portal. This stage often involves the payment of semester fees to complete the registration process.
5.  **Add/Drop Period:** Following main registration, a window is provided during which students can modify their course selections by adding new courses or dropping existing ones, subject to credit limits and availability.
6.  **Withdrawal Period:** A subsequent period allows students to withdraw from a registered course without academic penalty, though specific rules and implications apply.
7.  **Late Registration:** Students who miss the main registration deadline may be allowed to register during a late registration period, typically incurring a late fee.

Throughout these stages, students may consult with their faculty advisors for guidance on course selection and academic planning.
