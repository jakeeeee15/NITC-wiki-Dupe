# Academic Regulations of NIT Calicut

## Overview

The Academic Regulations of the National Institute of Technology Calicut (NIT Calicut) constitute a comprehensive set of rules and guidelines that govern all academic aspects of student life within the institution. These regulations are designed to ensure academic integrity, maintain high educational standards, provide a fair and transparent framework for student evaluation, and define the responsibilities of both students and the institution in the academic sphere.

Typically formulated and approved by the Academic Council and/or the Senate of NIT Calicut, these regulations cover a wide range of areas from admission to graduation. They are essential for students to understand their rights, responsibilities, and the procedures they must follow throughout their academic journey at the institute.

The specific and most current version of these regulations is officially published by NIT Calicut, usually available on the institute's official website or through the Academic Section. Students are advised to refer to the official document applicable to their specific admission year and program for precise details.

## Details

The Academic Regulations of NIT Calicut typically encompass, but are not limited to, the following key areas. Please note that the specific criteria, percentages, procedures, and values for each point are detailed exclusively in the official Academic Regulations document published by NIT Calicut and are not provided here due to the inability to access and verify the current, precise, and official regulations in real-time.

*   **Admission and Registration:** Rules pertaining to eligibility criteria, admission procedures, course registration, add/drop periods, and withdrawal from courses or programs.
*   **Course Structure and Credit System:** Details on the curriculum, credit requirements for various programs (B.Tech, M.Tech, MCA, MBA, Ph.D.), course numbering, prerequisites, and elective choices.
*   **Attendance Requirements:** Minimum attendance percentages required for eligibility to appear in examinations, and consequences of failing to meet these requirements.
*   **Examination and Evaluation Procedures:** Guidelines for continuous internal assessment, mid-semester examinations, end-semester examinations, practical examinations, project evaluations, and thesis submissions.
*   **Grading System:** Explanation of the grading scheme (e.g., letter grades, grade points), calculation of Semester Grade Point Average (SGPA) and Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA), and criteria for passing courses.
*   **Promotion and Degree Requirements:** Rules for promotion to the next academic year/semester, minimum CGPA requirements for continuation of study, and criteria for the award of degrees.
*   **Academic Probation and Discontinuation:** Policies regarding academic probation for students who do not meet minimum academic performance standards, and conditions under which a student may be required to discontinue their program.
*   **Academic Grievance Procedures:** Mechanisms for students to appeal against grades, evaluation decisions, or other academic matters.
*   **Academic Misconduct and Disciplinary Actions:** Definitions of academic misconduct (e.g., plagiarism, cheating) and the disciplinary actions that may be taken against students found guilty of such offenses.
*   **Leave Rules:** Regulations concerning various types of leave (e.g., medical leave, academic leave) and their impact on academic progression.
*   **Change of Branch/Program:** Criteria and procedures for students wishing to change their academic branch or program, if permitted.
*   **Re-evaluation and Supplementary Examinations:** Rules regarding re-evaluation of answer scripts and opportunities for supplementary examinations.

## Facilities

Academic regulations themselves are not physical facilities. However, their implementation and adherence are supported by various academic and administrative facilities and departments within NIT Calicut. These include:

*   **Academic Section/Dean (Academic Affairs) Office:** The central administrative body responsible for the formulation, dissemination, and enforcement of academic regulations, student registration, examination scheduling, and result processing.
*   **Department Offices:** Each academic department plays a role in implementing regulations specific to their courses and programs, including advising students, managing course registrations, and conducting evaluations.
*   **Examination Halls and Laboratories:** Physical spaces where examinations are conducted and practical components of courses are delivered, adhering to the rules outlined in the regulations.
*   **Library and Information Centre:** Provides resources essential for academic study and research, supporting students in meeting their academic requirements.
*   **Computer Centre:** Offers computational resources vital for coursework, projects, and research, often governed by specific usage policies that align with academic integrity.
