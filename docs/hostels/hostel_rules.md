# Hostel Rules at NIT Calicut

## Overview

Hostel rules at the National Institute of Technology Calicut (NIT Calicut) are established to ensure a disciplined, safe, and conducive living and learning environment for all resident students. These rules govern various aspects of hostel life, including entry/exit, visitor policies, mess conduct, maintenance of discipline, and the use of facilities. The administration of hostels and enforcement of rules falls under the purview of the Dean (Students Welfare), Chief Warden, and individual Wardens/Assistant Wardens of each hostel.

## Details

Specific hostel rules are typically detailed in the official Hostel Manual or Student Handbook provided to students upon admission or made available on the institute's official website. While comprehensive, publicly available, and exhaustive lists of all specific rules (e.g., exact timings for all hostels, detailed penalty structures) may vary in accessibility, the general principles include:

*   **Entry and Exit Timings:** Hostels generally have specified entry and exit timings, particularly for girls' hostels, to ensure student safety and accountability. Overnight leave from the hostel or campus typically requires prior permission from the respective Warden and, in many cases, parental consent.
*   **Visitor Policy:** Visitors are generally restricted from entering student rooms. Designated common areas or visitor lounges are usually provided for meeting visitors during specified hours.
*   **Mess Rules:** All resident students are typically required to join the hostel mess. Rules govern mess timings, conduct within the mess, and adherence to the mess schedule.
*   **Discipline and Conduct:**
    *   **Anti-Ragging Policy:** NIT Calicut maintains a strict anti-ragging policy in accordance with UGC regulations. Ragging in any form is prohibited and subject to severe disciplinary action.
    *   **Prohibited Substances:** The consumption or possession of alcoholic beverages, illegal drugs, tobacco products, and weapons within hostel premises is strictly prohibited.
    *   **Noise Levels:** Residents are expected to maintain decorum and keep noise levels to a minimum, especially during designated study hours.
    *   **Damage to Property:** Students are responsible for the proper upkeep of hostel property. Any damage caused to hostel infrastructure or furnishings may result in fines or disciplinary action.
*   **Electrical Appliances:** Restrictions are often placed on the use of high-wattage electrical appliances (e.g., electric heaters, induction cookers) in rooms due to safety concerns and electricity load.
*   **Cleanliness and Hygiene:** Residents are expected to maintain cleanliness in their rooms and contribute to the overall hygiene of the hostel premises.

## Facilities

The hostel facilities at NIT Calicut, which are subject to the established rules, generally include:

*   **Accommodation:** Separate hostels for male and female students, typically offering single or shared occupancy rooms.
*   **Mess:** Centralized dining facilities providing meals to residents.
*   **Common Rooms:** Areas for recreation, television, and social interaction.
*   **Internet Connectivity:** Wi-Fi or wired internet access is usually provided in hostels.
*   **Laundry Services:** Facilities for laundry, either through designated areas or outsourced services.
*   **Security:** Security personnel and surveillance systems are typically in place to ensure the safety of residents.
*   **Basic Amenities:** Water coolers, common washrooms, and basic maintenance services.
